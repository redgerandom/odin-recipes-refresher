Haitian Marinade

Description

Ann fè bon manje! Haitian Marinade is a beloved street food and snack in Haitian cuisine. These crispy, deep-fried fritters are made from a seasoned batter of flour, water, and aromatic spices, often with the addition of fresh herbs like parsley and thyme. Marinades are versatile and sometimes include vegetables, fish, or meat. They are golden and crunchy on the outside while soft and flavorful inside, making them an irresistible treat perfect for gatherings or as an appetizer.

Ingrediants

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper

Instructions

  1. Combine the flour, water, salt, pepper, and spices in a large mixing bowl. Mix until smooth and well combined.
  2. Add the chopped parsley and thyme to the batter and mix until evenly distributed.
  3. Heat oil in a deep fryer or large pot to 350°F.
  4. Using a spoon or scoop, carefully drop spoonfuls of the batter into the hot oil. Fry until golden brown and crispy, about 3-5 minutes.
  5. Remove the marinades from the oil and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  6. Serve hot and enjoy as a snack or appetizer.
