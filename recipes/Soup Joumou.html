Soup Joumou

Description

Ann fè bon manje! Soup Joumou is a traditional Haitian pumpkin soup made to celebrate Haitian Independence Day on January 1st. This hearty and flavorful dish is a symbol of freedom, resilience, and pride for Haitians, as it was historically denied to enslaved people. The soup features a rich blend of ingredients, including calabaza squash (or similar pumpkin), beef or other meats, root vegetables, pasta, and fragrant herbs and spices, creating a comforting and deeply nourishing meal.

Ingrediants

  • 1 Butternut squash about 2 ½ – 3 lbs.
  • 3 cups Macaroni
  • 1 lb. Beef cubes
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger chopped
  • 1 lb. Fresh vegetable 8 blend
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 Garlic cloves
  • Juice from 2 lemons or limes
  • 1 teaspoon Garden sage fresh and chopped
  • 3 cups Shredded cabbage
  • 1 tablespoon Better than bouillon vegetable paste
  • 2 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 tablespoon Fresh parsley
  • 10 cups Water plus extra for cooking
  • 2 cups Vegetable broth

Instructions

  1. Prepare the ingredients:
    • Peel and chop the calabaza squash (or pumpkin) into chunks.
    • Peel and chop your root vegetables (potatoes, carrots, yam, etc.).
    • Cut the beef (or preferred meat) into cubes.
    • Wash and chop the herbs (thyme, parsley, etc.).
  2. Cook the meat:
    • In a large pot, add the beef, garlic, onions, bell pepper, and seasonings (salt, pepper, paprika, etc.).
    • Brown the meat in a bit of oil for about 5 minutes. Add water to cover the meat, then simmer until tender (about 45 minutes).
  3. Prepare the squash and vegetables:
    • In a separate pot, bring water to a boil and add the squash and root vegetables. Cook until tender, approximately 30-40 minutes.
  4. Combine the soup:
    • Once the meat is tender, add the cooked vegetables and squash to the pot with the beef. Stir everything together, adding water to reach your desired consistency.
  5. Add pasta and simmer:
    • Add the pasta and cook for an additional 10-15 minutes until the pasta is tender.
  6. Final seasoning and serving:
    • Adjust seasoning with salt, pepper, and a touch of lime or lemon juice.
    • Serve hot, garnished with fresh parsley or thyme.
